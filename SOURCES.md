# Sources and Editorial Method

The Inner Monk is a formation tool, not an academic edition, official liturgical calendar, or substitute for parish teaching. The Father and saint profiles are concise summaries written for reflection. They should be checked against primary texts, Orthodox saint lives, and reputable translations before publication at scale.

## Primary Text Collections

- [New Advent: Church Fathers](https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/) - public-domain translations of many early Christian writings.
- [Christian Classics Ethereal Library](https://ccel.org/) - searchable public-domain Christian classics and patristic collections.
- [Orthodox Church in America: Lives of the Saints](https://www.oca.org/saints/lives) - Orthodox saint lives and liturgical commemorations.
- [Internet Archive](https://archive.org/) - digitized historical editions and older translations.

## Editorial Guardrails

- Scripture quotations in generated rules are restricted to sayings and teachings of Jesus from the Gospels.
- Historical dates and martyrdom details are presented cautiously when traditions differ.
- The Witness of the Day rotation is an MVP content rotation, not a live Orthodox calendar.
- Guidance on fasting, diet, exercise, compulsive behavior, or mental and physical health does not replace a physician, priest, pastoral guide, or qualified professional.
- Reflection prompts redirect attention toward Christ and concrete obedience without soliciting graphic details, diagnosis, or automated moral judgment.
- The Nicene-Constantinopolitan Creed remains central; the Apostles' Creed appears only as a historical Western note.
